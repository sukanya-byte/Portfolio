Project Overview

A platform for the way lending teams actually work.

Designed an enterprise CRM & field-ops system for an NBFC focused on lead intelligence, customer trust, disposition clarity, and end-to-end workflow visibility for sales and field agents.

Problem Statement
Lead management was fragmented across spreadsheets, call logs, WhatsApp conversations, and multiple internal systems. Agents struggled to track customer history, manage callbacks, assign opportunities, and maintain visibility into lead progress.
Solution
A unified CRM workspace that consolidates leads, customer interactions, call records, notes, dispositions, opportunities, assignments, and operational reporting into a single platform.
Key Design Focus
  • Conversation-first workflow rather than record-first workflow.
  • Unified customer timeline for complete interaction history.
  • Structured lead disposition framework.
  • Faster handoff between telecalling and relationship managers.
My Role
  • Led UX strategy and product design across CRM workflows.

  • Designed lead workspace, activity logging, reporting, and analytics modules.

Design Process

Impact

Business Challenge

Lead Management Challenges

📈 Growth without visibility

Lead acquisition was increasing across channels, but agents relied on spreadsheets, call logs, and disconnected portals to manage follow-ups. More leads entered the system than teams could effectively track.

🧩 Customer context disappeared

Every call, note, callback, and interaction lived in different places. Agents often started conversations without knowing what happened during the last customer interaction.

⏳ Decisions happened too late

Managers reviewed performance through static reports and spreadsheets generated after the fact. By the time issues became visible, opportunities had already gone cold.

OPERATIONAL FRICTION

Five frictions, repeated every day.

01

Which lead needs attention right now?

Agents managed hundreds of leads simultaneously, but there was no clear signal indicating priority. High-intent opportunities often looked identical to inactive leads.

02

What happened during the last interaction?

Call recordings, notes, WhatsApp conversations, and follow-up actions existed across different systems. Agents spent valuable time reconstructing customer history before every conversation.

03

Where is this lead in the journey?

Lead progress depended on multiple stakeholders and systems. Neither customers nor agents had a clear understanding of the current status or next expected action.

04

What does this disposition mean?

Different teams interpreted disposition codes differently. Similar outcomes were logged under different labels, creating inconsistencies in reporting and operational decision-making.

05

Am I progressing towards my target?

Agents could see assigned leads, but lacked a clear view of performance, conversion progress, and daily productivity indicators needed to manage their workload effectively.

CRM Experience

Designed around the questions managers ask every day.

Across Gold Loans, MSME Loans, and Micro-LAP products, the experience was rebuilt around the questions Managers asked most often: Which leads need attention? What happened with this customer? Is the team progressing? Where are opportunities getting stuck? What action should happen next?

Case · 01 Visibility

Managers couldn't see what needed attention.

Research showed managers spent significant time piecing together lead performance from multiple dashboards and reports before deciding where to focus.
The experience was redesigned to surface operational priorities, team performance, and pending actions in a single view.

DESIGN FOCUS
  • Visibility over volume
  • Priority-first dashboards
  • Operational awareness
Case · 02 Context

Customer context was scattered.

Interaction history, lead details, customer sentiment, and progress updates lived across different systems. Managers lacked the full story when reviewing a lead. The lead workspace consolidated everything into a single contextual view.

DESIGN FOCUS
  • Unified lead intelligence
  • Reduced context switching
  • Faster decision-making
Case · 03 Continuity

Important conversations disappeared after the call.

Callbacks, notes, recordings, and discussions were difficult to trace, making coaching and follow-ups inconsistent.
A structured activity timeline transformed every interaction into a visible and searchable record.

DESIGN FOCUS
  • Conversation continuity
  • Shared customer history
  • Interaction traceability
Case · 04 Consistency

Teams spoke different operational languages.

Disposition codes, follow-up actions, and lead outcomes varied across branches, creating inconsistencies in reporting and execution. The workflow was redesigned around structured actions and guided decision-making.

DESIGN FOCUS
  • Workflow standardisation
  • Consistent lead progression
  • Better reporting quality
Case · 05 Analytics

Performance was visible only in hindsight.

Managers relied on historical reports to understand outcomes, making it difficult to intervene at the right time. Analytics surfaces were designed to expose trends, bottlenecks, and conversion signals as they emerged.

DESIGN FOCUS
  • Actionable analytics
  • Conversion visibility
  • Operational insights
